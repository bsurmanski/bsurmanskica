title: Skateboard Types
---
Skateboards are great. They are a fun form of transportation that is much faster than walking and much more portable than a bike. Not all boards are created equal.

[QuickQuiver](https://quickquiver.com/2021/06/15/first-skateboards-for-each-category-2021/) has a breakdown of categories and recommendations for each category. Here are a few:
- Park / Trick
	A park or trick short board is usually a lightweight, thin popsicle deck with nose and tail and with TKP trucks and small hard wheels. This setup is optimized for riding in skateparks and doing tricks like ollies, kickflips, etc. While the wheels roll nicely on smooth park cement, this type of board is one of the worst for actual transportation as the small hard wheels roll poorly over regular road surfaces.

- Cruiser / Mini Cruiser
	A shortboard, with larger softer wheels meant for cruising around on roads. The board may be a bit bigger than a park deck for more comfortable standing. Usually TKP trucks. It usually has a tail for kick turns, tictacs, and manuals. Ollies are possible but harder due to the heavier wheels and deck. It's usually fairly compact and good for short trips.

- Cruiser Longboard
	Similar to a cruiser shortboard but optimized for longer distances. It'll have a bigger deck to 1) give a more comfortable standing 2) sometimes provide enough space for drop-mount trucks or a drop-deck. 3) allow for cutouts. The last two allow you to comfortably fit quite large wheels without wheelbite or an awkwardly high standing platform. A commuter longboard is a cruiser subset that aims to maximize the wheel size, while minimizing the deck height and length, and optimizes for medium low (15-25kph) speeds.

- Downhill
	A category of longboard meant to go fast. Stiff deck, Stable RKP trucks, and stiff bushing as steering inputs are amplified at speed. 

- Freeride
	A category of longboard meant for cruising but that can take hills, more meant for the fun of traversing the hill than going fast. Often set up with wheels meant to slide, and will often be smaller than a true cruiser. The deck will often have a drop for easier pushing and to make slide initiation easy.

- Dancer
	Longboard dancing is a weird hybrid of cruising and tricks, and the boards reflect that. Riders literally dance on their board, and as such need a large flat deck. This usually means top-mount to avoid lips that will catch your feet. Often there are sections with grip removed so the rider can spin and slide their feet. Riders will often do flatland tricks modified for the heavier board.

- Surfskate
	Surfskates are a style of shorter board designed to propel by "pumping". Originally they were designed to mimic surfing, for off season training, but there are a few different sub-styles that are unified around pumping and agility. Any board can pump, but surfskates are built to maximize this.
